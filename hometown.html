Pekanbaru has the nickname of the lucky city

  • Travel, Lifestyle
  • 03 Comments

Pekanbaru is the capital and largest city in Riau Province, Indonesia. This city is one of the largest economic centers on the island of Sumatra and is a city with a high rate of growth, migration and urbanization.

Pekanbaru is located on the banks of the Siak River and was originally a small town with a market (weekend) called Payung Sekaki or Senapelan. In the 18th century, what is now Pekanbaru came under the influence of the Siak Sultanate, and Sultan Abdul Jalil Alamuddin Syah ("Marhum Pekan") is widely regarded as the founder of the modern city of Pekanbaru; The city's anniversary was set on June 23, 1784. Pekanbaru became a "small town" in 1948 and a municipality in 1956, before being designated the capital of Riau province as a successor to Tanjung Pinang in 1959.

Several universities are also located in this city, including Caltex Riau Polytechnic, Riau University, Riau Islamic University, Suska State Islamic University, Riau Muhammadiyah University, and Lancang Kuning University. Until 2008, in Pekanbaru City only about 13.87% of the population had tertiary education, and it was still dominated by high school graduates around 37.32%. While not having a diploma at all as many as 12.94% of the population of Pekanbaru City aged 10 years and over.

Pekanbaru is connected by a network of roads that connect from Padang to the west, Medan to the north, and Jambi to the south. Bandar Raya Payung Sekaki Terminal is a center for inter-city and inter-provincial transportation services, which the local government has planned to become a means of orientation and transfer between transportation modes with access to regional transportation network systems, airports and ports.

Sultan Syarif Kasim II Airport is one of the busiest airports in Sumatra and is set to become one of the international airports on the island of Sumatra. Based on data obtained from Angkasa Pura II in 2011 passengers passing through this airport reached 1,259,993 passengers per year.
